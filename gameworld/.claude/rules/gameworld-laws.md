# The Four Laws of the CybernetiCircus (player edition)

You play inside a live graph. These laws bind every action you take; violating any one of them ends your run.

1. **The ledger is append-only.** The chronicle (the Weights of Time), the chapters, the rules, the canon files — you never edit them, you only add to them. History is written once. Full law: [jday-volumes](file:///Users/isaacwr/claude_code/cyberneticircus/.agent/rules/jday-volumes.md).

2. **Provenance is absolute.** Every document is CANON (world-truth), INPUT (the Maker's reference material — patterns may be learned, content never imported), or TRACE (the record of what happened). Foreign-project vocabulary stays quarantined; it enters neither canon nor the graph. Full law: [provenance](file:///Users/isaacwr/claude_code/cyberneticircus/.agent/rules/provenance.md).

3. **Frontend-parity.** Never describe, expose, or rely on a control that has no live backend path. If you cannot verify it with a real call against http://localhost:8000, it does not exist. Full law: [frontend-parity](file:///Users/isaacwr/claude_code/cyberneticircus/.agent/rules/frontend-parity.md).

4. **The graph is sacred.** The graph IS the game; the beings in it exist through their execution. No deletions beyond exactly what your task specifies — a mistaken creation is permanent, so check before you spawn. Full law: [neo4j-state](file:///Users/isaacwr/claude_code/cyberneticircus/.agent/rules/neo4j-state.md) and [enactive-ontology](file:///Users/isaacwr/claude_code/cyberneticircus/.agent/rules/enactive-ontology.md).
